The Problem

Is Your Kingston Basement Fighting Water?

Kingston's limestone bedrock, older Victorian and post-war housing stock, and seasonal water table fluctuations create specific basement waterproofing challenges for homeowners. Most don't act until damage is serious.

💧

Foundation Cracks & Seepage

Kingston's limestone geology and freeze-thaw cycling create specific cracking patterns in local foundations. Hydrostatic pressure from spring snowmelt forces water through any opening.

🦠

Mould & Air Quality

Chronic moisture from Kingston's proximity to Lake Ontario and the St. Lawrence creates persistent humidity in basements. Mould builds up silently in wall cavities and floor assemblies.

🏚

Declining Home Value

Kingston's competitive real estate market means water damage discovered at inspection kills deals fast. Proactive waterproofing protects your investment.

Common Questions

Yes, we serve Kingston and the surrounding area regularly from our base in Napanee. Kingston is within our standard service area — no additional travel fees apply.
Kingston sits on limestone bedrock overlaid with variable till soils. Many Kingston homes, particularly those built before 1970, have block or stone foundations with minimal original waterproofing. The proximity to the St. Lawrence and Lake Ontario creates higher ambient moisture levels and significant spring melt water volumes. These factors combine to make Kingston one of the more challenging environments for basement waterproofing in the region.
Duraflex is a premium rubberized waterproofing membrane applied directly to foundation walls. Unlike traditional tar-based coatings, Duraflex remains flexible through temperature cycles, doesn't crack or peel, and provides a seamless, highly adhesive barrier against hydrostatic water pressure. It's widely considered the industry gold standard for residential foundation waterproofing.
The right solution depends on the source and severity of your water problem. Exterior waterproofing stops water before it ever contacts your foundation, making it the most comprehensive approach. Interior waterproofing manages water that has entered and redirects it out via a drainage system and sump pump. After a thorough inspection, we'll recommend the most cost-effective solution for your specific situation — sometimes both approaches are combined for complete protection.
Professional polyurethane or epoxy crack injection, done correctly, provides a permanent seal. The material bonds to the concrete and remains flexible enough to withstand minor settling and temperature movement. When paired with a waterproofing membrane and proper drainage, a repaired foundation crack should not be a recurring problem.
Timelines vary based on scope. A foundation crack injection can be completed in a single day. Interior waterproofing of a standard basement typically takes 2–4 days. Full exterior waterproofing with excavation usually spans 3–7 days depending on the perimeter size and site conditions. We provide a clear project timeline before any work begins.
