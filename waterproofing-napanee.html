The Problem

Is Your Napanee Basement Leaking?

Greater Napanee sits on clay-heavy soil throughout Lennox & Addington County. This clay expands when saturated and contracts during dry periods, generating significant lateral and hydrostatic pressure against foundation walls year-round. Combined with Ontario's seasonal freeze-thaw cycle, this pressure forces water through the smallest foundation crack or joint in a foundation wall.

Spring snowmelt from the Napanee River watershed raises local water tables dramatically each year. Homes near Napanee Creek, the river flats, or low-lying areas of Greater Napanee face repeated flooding pressure below grade. Many homes in Greater Napanee were built between the 1960s and 1980s — meaning original waterproofing membranes, parging, and weeping tile systems are at or well past their expected service life.

Water doesn't just cause inconvenience. Left unchecked, wet basement moisture causes mould throughout wall cavities and HVAC systems, structural deterioration of foundation materials, framing rot when water reaches wood, and reduced property values. The earlier you address it, the less it costs to fix permanently.
